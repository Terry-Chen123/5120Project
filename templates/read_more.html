    Pneumonia Symptoms on Children

    • has a cough and fever
    • be short of breath or have difficulty breathing
    • sharp chest pains when breathing deeply or coughing
    • stomach ache and/or vomit
    • be irritable or tired
    • lose appetite
      • A younger baby with pneumonia might just look very ill, have a fever, and breathe rapidly, without coughing or showing the other symptoms above.


      Is Pneumonia Contagious?


      In general, pneumonia is not contagious, but the upper respiratory viruses and bacteria that lead to it are.

      When these germs are in fluid from the mouth or nose of someone who's infected, that person can spread the illness through coughs and sneezes. While many cases of pneumonia can be mild, left untreated some cases of pneumonia can be serious and even life-threatening. Sharing cups and utensils, touching the used tissues or handkerchiefs of an infected person also can spread pneumonia. So, it's best to keep kids away from anyone with symptoms of a respiratory infection.

  • Make sure you regularly wash hands with warm, soapy water;
  • Keep your hands away from your face as much as possible;
  • Get enough rest and do exercise
