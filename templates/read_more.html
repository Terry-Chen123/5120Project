    Introduction of pneumonia :

    • Has a cough and fever
    • Be short of breath or have difficulty breathing
    • Stomach ache and/or vomit
    • Be irritable or tired & lose appetite

    • In general, pneumonia is not contagious, but the upper   respiratory viruses and bacteria that lead to it are.
    • When these germs are in fluid from the mouth or nose of
      someone who's infected, that person can spread the illness   through coughs and sneezes.
    • While many cases of pneumonia can be mild, left untreated some   cases of pneumonia can be serious and even life-threatening.
      Sharing cups and utensils, touching the used tissues or

    It's best to keep kids away from anyone with symptoms of a respiratory infection.

    A child is more likely to get pneumonia if he or she:
    • Has weak immune system, such as from cancer
    • Has a chronic healthy problem, such as asthma or cystic fibrosis
    • Has problems with the lungs or airways
    • Is around second-hand tobacco smoke

    Prevention of Pneumonia :

    • Covers 13 strains of pneumococcal disease.
    • Helps to protect people from certain   infectious diseases.
    • Recommended for kids from 6 weeks to 17 years of age.
    • Children under 5 years old need 3 or 4 doses of Prevenar 13.
    • Recommended for adults older than 50 years.

    • Covers 23 strains of pneumococcal disease.
    • Provided free under the National Immunisation Program (NIP) for:   All children at 2, 4 and 12 months of age.
    • An extra dose at 6 months for Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander   children:  live in Queensland, Northern Territory, Western
      Australia or South Australia.

    Children of all ages who have certain medical conditions that put them at higher risk of getting serious pneumococcal disease.

    • Make sure you regularly wash hands with warm, soapy water
    • Keep your hands away from your face as much as possible
    • Get enough rest and do moderate exercise
    • Cough or sneeze into a tissue or your elbows.

    Treatment of Pneumonia :

    If your child's pneumonia is caused by bacteria, they will be prescribed antibiotics.


    • In mild cases, medicine can be taken orally at home.
    • Children usually start antibiotics within 48 hours.
    • Important to complete the whole course of antibiotics.
    • Treatment will continue for 3 to 7 days.
    • May continue to cough for up to three weeks after treatment.
    • Children who are very unwell may be admitted to the hospital
      for antibiotics given directly.
    • Some children may also need oxygen or extra fluids.

    • Usually not as severe as bacterial pneumonia.
    • Recovery can be slower, taking up to four weeks.
    • Antibiotics do not cure viruses and are not given for viral   pneumonia.

    • Take your child to the GP as soon as possible.
    • Make sure your child gets as much rest as possible.
    • Cover the mouth with tissue or elbows when sneezing or
      coughing to avoid the spread of the viruses or bacteria.
    • Keep your home smoke free. Smoke can make pneumonia worse.
    • Don’t get pneumonia and the flu vaccine at the same time,
      this may increase the risk of children having seizures.
    • Your children’s appetite will improve they start to feel better.

    It’s very important to make sure your child is drinking enough so they don’t get dehydrated

    • Breastfed babies younger than six months
    • Formula-fed babies younger than six months
    • Breastfed babies older than six months

    Offer feeds more often and offer water between feeds.

    • Formula-fed babies older than six months

    Offer smaller but more frequent feeds.

    • Children older than one year.

    1. Offer your child water or an oral rehydration fluid.
    2. You can buy oral rehydration fluid from pharmacies and
        supermarkets.
    3. Give your child smaller amounts of water or oral rehydration fluid
        frequently, especially if your child is really unwell and has been
        vomiting.
    4. You might need to offer a few mouthfuls every 15 minutes.
